My Story

Want to know where it all started? The year was 2015 and I was doing computer science in elementary school. All students were supposed to create one Scratch coding project that would utilize one basic coding mechanic. However, I found this idea a bit boring and wanted to do something more. A day later, I posted "The makkara game" (the sausage game), which combined my humour with my passion for ice hockey. The project got praise from all my peers, and I got full marks on the course.

After that, I attended the IB program for high school (it had the nickname "killer high school"), and I also stopped coding. I wanted to study psychology but got into business school with my diploma. During university, I decided to take coding classes, since they challenged me more than other courses, and I was able to be more creative. I loved the challenges and the feeling after solving complex issues. If there was a concept that I didn't understand, I practiced until I understood it fully.

And now here I am, recently graduated from University but still wanting to do more and more coding. Because of my passion I have been able to use HTML and CSS at my current position at work, but I want to take my skills further and get more into Full-end instead of purely front-end. My philosophy still is to grind the concepts I feel are most challenging, until I master them.
